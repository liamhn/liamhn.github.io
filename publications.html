Using computational modeling techniques to explain fluorescence changes of fluorescent proteins in crowded environments

Tools used: CHARMMGUI, GROMACS, Python, Open3D, Seaborn, Numpy, Pandas, VMD, MDTRAJ, MDAnalysis, SciPy


Joron, K., Viegas, J.O., Haas-Neill, L. et al (2023). Fluorescent protein lifetimes report densities and phases of nuclear condensates during embryonic stem-cell differentiation. Nature Communications. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-023-40647-6


My contribution to this project was to perform molecular simulations to explain the differences in fluorscence lifetime observed in experimental data when mCherry was immersed in a crowded solution. Statistical analysis of simulations of mCherry in water and in polyethylene glycol indicated small but significant changes in the structure near the fluorphore which could explain the changes in fluorescence lifetime.
